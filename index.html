About

A Life of
Diplomatic Service

Bhupinder Singh Liddar is a Kenya-born retired Canadian diplomat who has dedicated over four decades to public service, international diplomacy, and media. He began his distinguished career on Parliament Hill in Ottawa in 1977, where he served until 1988.

Known for his eloquence and commitment to intercultural dialogue, Bhupinder has played a pivotal role in shaping Canada's diplomatic narrative — particularly in its relationship with South Asia and the broader Commonwealth nations.

His career spans Parliament Hill, flagship media platforms, and high-level diplomatic posts, culminating in his appointment as Consul General of Canada in Chandigarh, India in 2004, and his role as UNEP Deputy Ambassador — representing Canada on the world environmental stage.

Career Journey

Four Decades of
Distinguished Service

A timeline of milestones that shaped a remarkable diplomatic career

1977
Parliamentary Staff
Parliament Hill, Ottawa
Began a distinguished career on Parliament Hill, working at the heart of Canada's federal government for over a decade, gaining deep expertise in public policy and international affairs.
1988
Founder & Publisher
Diplomat Magazine
Established Diplomat Magazine, Canada's foremost publication covering international affairs, foreign policy, and the Ottawa diplomatic community — building a vital bridge between government and civil society.
1990s
Columnist
Ottawa Citizen
Authored an influential weekly column on diplomatic affairs and Canada-India relations in the Ottawa Citizen, reaching a broad national readership with incisive geopolitical analysis.
1990s–2000s
Television Host
Diplomatic World TV (CPAC)
Hosted the acclaimed Diplomatic World television programme on CPAC (Cable Public Affairs Channel), interviewing global leaders, ambassadors, and policy makers on international affairs.
2004
Consul General of Canada
Chandigarh, India
Appointed Canada's Consul General in Chandigarh, representing Canadian interests across Punjab, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, and Jammu & Kashmir — a landmark posting strengthening bilateral ties.
Post-2004
Deputy Ambassador
UNEP — United Nations Environment Programme
Served as UNEP Deputy Ambassador, representing Canada in multilateral environmental negotiations and championing sustainable development on the world stage.
